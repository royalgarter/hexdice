# **\[v1.5\] Epic Chess Mode**

## 

This "Epic Chess Mode" variant is designed for players seeking an expanded, deeply strategic, and longer-form Hex Dice Tactics experience, moving beyond the casual quick-play. It features a fixed, symmetrical army composition and emphasizes grand tactical planning and unit management.

## **1\. Overview** 

This variant introduces a fixed, symmetrical army for each player, creating a setup that evokes classic chess, but on a Hex Dice Tactics battlefield. The game remains a tactical combat simulation with deterministic combat and evolving terrain, now with a significantly larger force.

## **2\. Components**

* **1 Hexagonal Map (R=6 or R=8):** An R=8 map provides sufficient space for the larger armies and tactical maneuvers.  
* **Standard 6-sided dice:** Two distinct colors (e.g., Red/Blue).  
  * **For 2 Players: 2 sets of 21 dice each.** Each set must contain a fixed distribution of dice values.

## **3\. Setup**

1. ### **Place the Hexagonal Map** in the center of the playground.

2. ### **Fixed army composition**
  * ### **21 dice total per player**
    * Dice 1: 6 units
    * Dice 2: 5 units
    * Dice 3: 4 units
    * Dice 4: 3 units
    * Dice 5: 2 units
    * Dice 6: 1 units
  * The dice are placed showing their designated face value face up.

3. ### **Deployment:** 
  * The two players occupy opposite corner hexes. Each player's deployment area is a large triangular wedge extending from their chosen corner.
  * Players symmetrically deploy their 21 units onto any empty hexes within their defined Deployment Area following the diagram below or strategically (based on players' consensus). Each hex can initially hold only one unit.

## **4\. Gameplay Modifications & Interactions**

* **Game Length & Scale:** With 42 units on the board, this mode will cause significantly longer game sessions (likely 3-4+ hours). It shifts the game from a skirmish to a grand tactical war of attrition.  
* **Army Composition:** The fixed army removes the initial randomness of unit rolls, creating a symmetrical and balanced starting point for pure strategic play. Players will need to master each dice type's strengths and weaknesses.  
* **Actions:**  
  * **The Reroll action is DISABLED in Epic Chess Mode.** Units cannot use this action.  
  * **The Merging \[v1.1\] action remains ENABLED in Epic Chess Mode.** Merging is a vital strategic tool for unit consolidation, creating powerful new units from weaker ones, and adapting your army composition throughout the long game.  
  * All other actions (Move, Guard, Ranged Attack, Special Attack, Terraform) function as per the base game's latest rules.  
* **Dice 6** (Could combine with **Terrain ability on v1.2, v1.4** depending on game configuration): The Dice 6 unit's abilities (Melee Attack with Conditional Movement on win, and Terraform Hex) become central to shaping the battlefield. Its ability to clear paths, create defensive Forests, or push through enemy lines by conquering hexes will be vital for breaking stalemates in a large-scale engagement.  
* **Fog of War (Combine with v1.3):** With 21 units per side, a large portion of the map will be revealed much faster. The FoW mechanic will primarily focus on concealing enemy units as they move out of visible range, adding tactical uncertainty rather than exploration of an unknown map.

## **5\. Winning the Game** 

The winning conditions remain as defined for multiplayer games: A player is eliminated if all their units are removed or an opponent occupies their Base corner hex. The game ends when only one player has units remaining on the board; that player is the winner.
