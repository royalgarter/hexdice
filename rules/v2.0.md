# Hex Dice Devlog: Introducing Version 2 - Destiny Dice

The battlefield is evolving. While **Decisive Dice** (Version 1) brought deterministic, chess-like precision to the hex grid, **Destiny Dice** (Version 2) introduces a layer of organized chaos, shifting the game from pure calculation to tactical risk management.

## The Winds of Fate: Two-Phase Turn Structure

Version 2 completely overhauls how you command your forces. A turn is split into two distinct phases:

### Phase 1: Fate's Call
At the start of every turn, a **Fate Die (1D6)** is rolled. Every friendly unit on the board whose value matches this roll gains a **free Move action**.
- **Swarm Mentality:** Since multiple units move for "free," the game now rewards Group Formations. The AI prioritizes creating defensive walls or surrounding high-value targets during this phase.
- **Setup Play:** The goal isn't necessarily to kill, but to reposition units into "Lethal Zones" where a Phase 2 unit can secure a base or kill with maximum support.

### Phase 2: Tactical Command
After the Fate moves, you perform a standard action (Move, Attack, Guard, or Spell) with **exactly one** unit. This is your deliberate, tactical choice.

## From Calculation to Probability: The New Combat Math

In Version 1, combat was deterministic. In Version 2, it is a calculated gamble:

**Total ATK = ceil((Unit ATK + Combat Roll) / 2)**

- **The Roll:** Every attack triggers a 1D6 combat roll. If `Total ATK > Defender Armor`, the target is destroyed.
- **The Fumble:** Beware the roll of **1**. If an attack fails (Total ATK <= Armor) on a roll of 1, the attacker is **immediately destroyed**.
- **Risk Assessment:** High-value units (like Knights) are now more fragile. The AI has evolved to become more risk-averse with its elites, often using "fodder" (Fencers/Archers) to take risky 50/50 gambles.

## Randomized Spells: The Oracle's Chaos

The Oracle (D6) has seen their power restricted. They now channel a random spell:
- **1 or 4:** Shield
- **2 or 5:** Swap
- **3 or 6:** Skirmish

The AI has moved from proactive spellcasting to **Adaptive Spellcasting**. It won't decide "I want to cast Shield." Instead, it evaluates the board for all three possibilities, activating the Oracle only if the average utility of the potential spells is high. If a spell like "Swap" would be disastrous, it will often forgo the Oracle entirely to avoid friendly fire.

## AI Evolution: Thinking in Expected Value (EV)

To keep up with Version 2, the AI has been upgraded to a "Gambler-Tactician." It no longer asks "Can I kill this?" but rather:
**Expected Value = (Win Prob * Target Value) - (Fumble Prob * Self Value)**

### AI "Personalities" in V2
| Personality | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Gambler** | Always takes the shot if P(Win) > 30%. Ignores Fumble risks. |
| **The Tactician** | Only attacks if P(Win) > 70%. Uses Phase 1 to build "Bodyguard" walls. |
| **The Chaos Lord** | Prioritizes Oracles. Relies on "luck-sacking" the right spell. |
| **The Conservative** | Only uses Phase 2 for "Guard" or "Base Defense" unless a 100% win is available. |

## Playtest Findings

During our simulations, we've observed that:
1. **D5 (Tanks)** are significantly more terrifying due to their high armor, making them hard to deflect.
2. **Phase 1 maneuvers** often determine the winner, with "burst movement" effectively doubling a unit's threat range.
3. **Fumbles** are common enough that every roll feels significant, creating dramatic swings where a weak defender can accidentally destroy an attacker.

### Fix 1: Solving the "Spam Attack" Problem
*The Problem: Without an armor reduction penalty, weak units can just throw themselves at strong units every turn with zero risk, hoping for a lucky roll.*

**Rex (Wargamer):** "If we want quick and fun, we don't want to track 'exhaustion' tokens or calculate pushback distances. We need high drama! I propose **The Fumble**. If you attack and roll a natural '1' on your Combat Dice, the gods have forsaken you. Your attacking unit trips, drops their guard, and is instantly destroyed by the defender."
**Alexei (Chess Purist):** "I actually agree with this. It introduces a flat 16.6% chance of death every time a weak unit tries to 'fish' for a lucky kill against a superior foe. It forces the attacker to ask: *Is this attack worth the risk of losing my unit?* It restores balance and protects defensive formations."

**The Rule Change:** **The Fumble.** If an attack fails, nothing happens—*unless* the attacker rolled a '1' on the Combat Dice. If a '1' is rolled, the attacking unit is destroyed. 

### Fix 2: Solving the "Spell Chaos" Problem
*The Problem: Rolling a 'Swap' spell when you desperately needed a 'Shield' can accidentally teleport your Oracle into a lethal trap, making magic feel too punishing.*

**Elena (Eurogamer):** "The fix here is incredibly simple: **Reverse the order of operations.** In the previous version, you picked the target, then rolled the spell. That causes frustration. Instead, the Oracle should roll the Spell Dice *first* to see what the gods grant them. *Then*, the player looks at the board and chooses the best target for that specific spell."
**Alexei (Chess Purist):** "Brilliant. This changes the Oracle from a random liability into a tactical puzzle. You roll a Swap? Now you get to look at the board and say, *'Okay, how can I use a Swap to my advantage right now?'* It keeps the 'true random' fate, but gives the player the agency to use it smartly."

**The Rule Change:** **Channel First, Target Second.** The Oracle rolls the Spell Dice (Modulo 3). Once the spell is revealed, the Oracle may apply it to *any* valid friendly unit in range. If no target is valid or desired, the spell simply fizzles (no penalty).

### Fix 3: A Bonus Tweak for "Quickness"
**Elena (Eurogamer):** "If we want the game to be truly fast, we should swap the order of the Turn Phases. Roll the Fate Dice *before* you take your Tactical Command."
**Rex (Wargamer):** "Oh, I love that! You roll the Fate Dice, suddenly three of your Archers move across the board. *Then* you take your Tactical Command to capitalize on the new board state. It's so much faster because you don't have to guess what the gods will do—you see what they do, and then you plan your main move."

### The Final, Polished Ruleset (The "Fate & Tactics" Edition)

Here is the complete, streamlined package ready for your rulebook:

**1. The Turn Flow (Fast & Dynamic)**
*   **Phase 1: Fate's Call.** Roll the Fate Dice (1d6). *All* friendly units on the board matching that number immediately get a free Move Action.
*   **Phase 2: Tactical Command.** Choose exactly *one* friendly unit. It may perform any standard action (Move, Attack, Guard, Spell). *(Yes, a unit that moved in Phase 1 can be chosen here to act again!)*

**2. Combat (Risky & Rewarding)**
*   **The Math:** `Total ATK = (Unit Base ATK + Combat Dice Roll) / 2`. *(Always round up!)*
*   **The Clash:** 
    *   If Total ATK > Defender's Effective Armor: Defender is destroyed.
    *   If Total ATK ≤ Defender's Effective Armor: Attack is deflected.
*   **The Fumble:** If the attack is deflected AND the Combat Dice roll was a '1', the attacking unit is destroyed!

**3. Magic (Chaotic but Tactical)**
*   **Channeling:** To cast a spell, the Oracle rolls the Spell Dice.
*   **The Weave (Modulo 3):**
    *   Roll 1 or 4 (Result 1) = **Shield** (+2 Guard Charges)
    *   Roll 2 or 5 (Result 2) = **Swap** (Oracle and target swap places)
    *   Roll 3 or 6 (Result 0) = **Skirmish** (Grants Hit & Run buff)
*   **Targeting:** *After* the spell is revealed, the Oracle chooses one friendly unit within Range 2 to receive the spell.

**Why this works:** You have successfully transformed Hex Dice. It is now a fast-paced, highly dramatic game. The math is simple (divide by 2, round up), the turns are explosive (Fate first, then Tactics), and every dice roll—whether for combat or magic—will have players holding their breath!
