
### Fix 1: Solving the "Spam Attack" Problem
*The Problem: Without an armor reduction penalty, weak units can just throw themselves at strong units every turn with zero risk, hoping for a lucky roll.*

**Rex (Wargamer):** "If we want quick and fun, we don't want to track 'exhaustion' tokens or calculate pushback distances. We need high drama! I propose **The Fumble**. If you attack and roll a natural '1' on your Combat Dice, the gods have forsaken you. Your attacking unit trips, drops their guard, and is instantly destroyed by the defender."
**Alexei (Chess Purist):** "I actually agree with this. It introduces a flat 16.6% chance of death every time a weak unit tries to 'fish' for a lucky kill against a superior foe. It forces the attacker to ask: *Is this attack worth the risk of losing my unit?* It restores balance and protects defensive formations."

**The Rule Change:** **The Fumble.** If an attack fails, nothing happens—*unless* the attacker rolled a '1' on the Combat Dice. If a '1' is rolled, the attacking unit is destroyed. 

### Fix 2: Solving the "Spell Chaos" Problem
*The Problem: Rolling a 'Swap' spell when you desperately needed a 'Shield' can accidentally teleport your Oracle into a lethal trap, making magic feel too punishing.*

**Elena (Eurogamer):** "The fix here is incredibly simple: **Reverse the order of operations.** In the previous version, you picked the target, then rolled the spell. That causes frustration. Instead, the Oracle should roll the Spell Dice *first* to see what the gods grant them. *Then*, the player looks at the board and chooses the best target for that specific spell."
**Alexei (Chess Purist):** "Brilliant. This changes the Oracle from a random liability into a tactical puzzle. You roll a Swap? Now you get to look at the board and say, *'Okay, how can I use a Swap to my advantage right now?'* It keeps the 'true random' fate, but gives the player the agency to use it smartly."

**The Rule Change:** **Channel First, Target Second.** The Oracle rolls the Spell Dice (Modulo 3). Once the spell is revealed, the Oracle may apply it to *any* valid friendly unit in range. If no target is valid or desired, the spell simply fizzles (no penalty).

### The Final, Polished Ruleset (The "Fate & Tactics" Edition)

Here is the complete, streamlined package ready for your rulebook:

**1. The Turn Flow (Fast & Dynamic)**
*   **Phase 1: Fate's Call.** Roll the Fate Dice (1d6). *All* friendly units on the board matching that number immediately get a free Move Action.
*   **Phase 2: Tactical Command.** Choose exactly *one* friendly unit. It may perform any standard action (Move, Attack, Guard, Spell). *(Yes, a unit that moved in Phase 1 can be chosen here to act again!)*

**2. Combat (Risky & Rewarding)**
*   **The Math:** `Total ATK = (Unit Base ATK + Combat Dice Roll) / 2`. *(Always round up!)*
*   **The Clash:** 
    *   If Total ATK > Defender's Effective Armor: Defender is destroyed.
    *   If Total ATK ≤ Defender's Effective Armor: Attack is deflected.
*   **The Fumble:** If the attack is deflected AND the Combat Dice roll was a '1', the attacking unit is destroyed!

**3. Magic (Chaotic but Tactical)**
*   **Channeling:** To cast a spell, the Oracle rolls the Spell Dice.
*   **The Weave (Modulo 3):**
    *   Roll 1 or 4 (Result 1) = **Shield** (+2 Guard Charges)
    *   Roll 2 or 5 (Result 2) = **Swap** (Oracle and target swap places)
    *   Roll 3 or 6 (Result 0) = **Skirmish** (Grants Hit & Run buff)
*   **Targeting:** *After* the spell is revealed, the Oracle chooses one friendly unit within Range 2 to receive the spell.

**Why this works:** You have successfully transformed Hex Dice. It is now a fast-paced, highly dramatic game. The math is simple (divide by 2, round up), the turns are explosive (Fate first, then Tactics), and every dice roll—whether for combat or magic—will have players holding their breath!
