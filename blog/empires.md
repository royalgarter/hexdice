# v1.6 Empires Mode: The Global Six

Version 1.6 introduces "Empires Mode," adding a layer of strategic depth and asymmetry to "Hex Dice Tactics." Players now choose an Empire before deployment, granting a unique, permanent buff to one specific type of Dice Soldier throughout the game. This encourages varied playstyles and adds a significant decision point in the setup phase.

**Rationale for Empires Mode:**
*   **Asymmetry:** Introduces unique strengths for each player, moving beyond symmetrical starting armies.
*   **Strategic Choice:** Adds a meaningful decision during setup, allowing players to tailor their strategy to their chosen Empire's buff.
*   **Replayability:** With 6 distinct Empires, each game can feel different based on the chosen factions and their interactions.
*   **Thematic Depth:** Connects the abstract dice units to iconic historical civilizations.

---

### **1. Overview**

Hex Dice Tactics now includes an optional "Empires Mode" where players select a unique Empire at the start of the game. Each Empire bestows a powerful, permanent buff to one specific Dice Soldier unit type, influencing tactical decisions from setup to victory.

### **2. Setup & Selection**

1.  **Determine Your Army:** Each player rolls all their assigned dice simultaneously to determine their unit composition.
2.  **Empire Selection:** After rolling dice, but *before* deploying units, players choose their Empire.
    *   *Casual Play:* Players can simply choose their favorite.
    *   *Competitive Play:* Players roll a die; highest roll picks first, then selection proceeds clockwise.
3.  **Deployment:** Players deploy their units as per standard rules. The chosen Empire's buff immediately applies to all units of that type.

---

### **3. The Global Six Empires**

Each Empire provides a unique, independent buff to a specific unit type.

| Empire | Unit Type | Buff Name | Effect |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Aztecs** | **D1 (Fencer)** | *Jarguar Warrior* | **+1 Armor.** Base Armor increases from 2 to 3. |
| **Britons** | **D2 (Archer)** | *Longbowmen* | **+1 Range.** Range increases from 2 to 1-3. (Range 3 attacks suffer -1 Power). |
| **Mongols** | **D3 (Hussar)** | *Nomadic Raid* | **Hit & Run.** After winning a melee combat, the unit may immediately move 1 hex to an adjacent empty hex. |
| **Japanese** | **D4 (Knight)** | *Shinobi Dodge* | **Evasion.** When attacked, attacker rolls 1D6. On a **1 or 2**, the attack misses completely (no damage/AR to either side). |
| **Romans** | **D5 (Tanker)** | *Testudo March* | **Auto-Guard.** After performing a Move action, this unit automatically enters **Guard Mode** (+1 Shield Charge). |
| **Egyptians** | **D6 (Oracle)** | *Pharaoh’s Reach* | **Spell Range.** All Spellcast actions (Shield, Swap, Skirmish) have their range increased from 2 to **3 hexes**. |

### **4. Expert Commentary & Balance**

<expert name="Richard Garfield" field="Game Mechanics & Systems">The **Roman Tanker** is the most significant shift. Since Tankers reflect damage while Guarding, a Roman Tanker becomes a moving hazard that melee units struggle to approach. However, their slow movement (Distance 1) keeps them manageable via ranged fire or strategic kiting.</expert>

<expert name="Eric Lang" field="Tactical Game Design">The **Japanese Knight** adds a layer of psychological warfare. Even a high-attack unit might hesitate to engage a Shinobi Knight because of that 33% dodge chance. It makes the diagonal-moving unit even more elusive and difficult to pin down.</expert>

<expert name="Jamey Stegmaier" field="Board Game Design">The **Aztec Fencer** buff is simple but creates a very sturdy frontline. Having "Mini-Tanks" (Armor 3) that can move 2 hexes in any direction makes the Aztecs the most reliable choice for new players or those who prefer a solid, aggressive advance.</expert>

---

### **5. Gameplay Notes**

*   **Merging:** If a player merges units to create a unit type buffed by their Empire, the new unit immediately receives the buff.
*   **Independent:** These buffs function independently of Terrain, Fog of War, or Terraform features.
*   **Version Compatibility:** These buffs are compatible with both **Version 1 (Decisive)** and **Version 2 (Destiny)** combat modes. For the Japanese Dodge in Version 2, the dodge roll occurs *before* the attacker rolls their Combat Die.
