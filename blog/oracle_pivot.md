# From Immortal Walls to Tactical Playmakers: The Oracle Pivot

In our latest development cycle for Hex Dice, we tackled one of the most persistent "fun-killers" in our early playtests: the **Unit 6 Stalemate**. 

This is the story of how we pivoted from a design of pure mathematical defense to a design of high-stakes strategic utility.

## The Problem: The "Immortal 6" Crisis

In the legacy v1.0 and v1.4 rules, Unit 6 (the Legate/Legion) was designed as a "Defensive Anchor." It had the highest Armor in the game (6) and provided a passive +1 Armor aura to all adjacent friendly units.

On paper, this sounds like a great reward for a high roll. In practice, it created a **"Stat-Check" nightmare**:
*   **Mathematical Invincibility:** If a player lost their high-attack units (Dice 4 or 5), they literally could not deal enough damage to kill a Dice 6. The game would grind to a halt.
*   **Static Gameplay:** The best strategy was to "castle" up—park the 6 on a base and never move it. It was effective, but it wasn't *fun*. It turned a tactical battle into a boring math problem.

## The Solution: The Oracle (v1.5 Revamp)

We realized that for Hex Dice to thrive, every unit needs to be interactive. We decided to strip away the "tankiness" of Unit 6 and replace it with **Creative Agency**. 

Enter **The Oracle**.

### The 0-1-1-2-3 Mnemonic
To make the new unit easy to remember, we designed it around a simple numerical sequence:
*   **0** Attack (It cannot kill anything on its own).
*   **1** Armor (It is the most fragile unit on the board).
*   **1** Distance (It moves slow).
*   **2** Range (For its spells).
*   **3** Spells in its kit.

## Tactical Magic: The 3-Spell Kit

Instead of a passive aura, the Oracle now requires an **Activation**. You have to *choose* to use the Oracle instead of attacking with another unit. This adds a layer of "Action Economy" that was missing before.

1.  **Shield (Remote Guard):** The Oracle can grant **Guard Mode** (+1 Armor) to a friendly unit from 2 hexes away. This lets you protect your front line without putting your support unit in danger.
2.  **Swap (The Great Repositioner):** This is the Oracle’s most powerful tool. It allows the Oracle to exchange positions with a friendly unit. 
    *   *The Save:* Pull a wounded Knight out of the fire.
    *   *The Warp:* Warp a slow, heavy Tank (Unit 5) across a chasm into the enemy’s face.
3.  **Mend (Armor Repair):** The Oracle can remove Armor Reduction from a friendly unit, sustaining your heavy hitters against the "chip damage" of smaller Pawns.

## Why This Works

The pivot to the Oracle fixes the "Immortal 6" problem by making the most valuable unit also the most vulnerable. 

The Oracle is a **"Glass Support."** It is a high-priority target that must be protected, but if utilized correctly, its ability to manipulate the board state (especially with **Swap**) creates "hero moments" that a static +1 Armor buff never could.

We've moved from a game where the winner is the one with the biggest numbers, to a game where the winner is the one with the best plan.

*The Oracle sees the path to victory—will you follow it?*
