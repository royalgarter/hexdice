General Combat Balance Analysis
  The current v1.0 system uses a "Stat-Check" hierarchy. Since Attack = Dice + 1 and Armor = Dice, a unit can
  always defeat an enemy of equal or lower value. However, a unit generally cannot defeat an enemy that is 2 or
  more levels higher (e.g., a Dice 1 cannot harm a Dice 3). While Armor Reduction exists for failed attacks,
  the single-activation-per-turn structure makes this attrition-based strategy very slow and often ineffective
  against high-value units.

  ---

  Unit-by-Unit Review


  Dice 1 (Pawn/Infantry)
   * Balance: High mobility (Move 3) but low combat power. It is an excellent harasser and base-capper but
     becomes a "dead piece" once the board is filled with Armor 3+ units.
   * Issue: It currently bounces harmlessly off half the units in the game. It needs the "Momentum" or "David &
     Goliath" adjustments proposed in the backlog to remain relevant in the mid-to-late game.


  Dice 2 (Bishop/Copter)
   * Balance: A versatile "middle-weight." Its diagonal movement on a hex grid allows it to bypass frontlines.
     With Attack 3, it is the lowest unit capable of threatening a Dice 3.
   * Issue: Like Dice 1, it suffers from the hierarchy gap against heavy Armor (4-6).


  Dice 3 (Knight/Assault)
   * Balance: The most balanced tactical piece. Jumping over friendly units is essential for maneuvering in
     tight spaces. Its Armor 3 is a critical breakpoint, making it immune to one-shot kills from Dice 1.
   * Issue: It is the "standard" by which other units are measured.


  Dice 4 (Rook/Cavalry)
   * Balance: A powerful "heavy" unit. Attack 5 kills almost everything except a Dice 6. Its lower mobility
     (Move 2) forces it to be played more methodically.
   * Issue: Often outmaneuvered by the high-mobility Dice 1-3.


  Dice 5 (Archer/Artillery)
   * Balance: The strongest unit in v1.0. With Attack 6 (lethal to all) and Range 2-3, it can dominate the
     board from safety.
   * Issue: Its "Dead Zone" (cannot attack adjacent units) and Move 1 are its only saving graces. A player with
     multiple 5s has a massive statistical advantage.


  Dice 6 (Legion/Engineer)
   * Balance: The ultimate defensive anchor. Its +1 Armor buff to neighbors creates "castled" positions that
     are nearly impenetrable.
   * Issue: The "Immortal 6" Problem. If an opponent loses their 5s and 6s, a Dice 6 (Armor 6) becomes
     mathematically unkillable. This can lead to stalemate scenarios where a player cannot be finished off.

  ---

  Strategic Summary & Risks


   1. The Hierarchy Gap: The game currently rewards "High Rolls" during setup too heavily. A player who rolls
      mostly 4s, 5s, and 6s will likely steamroll a player who rolled 1s and 2s, despite the latter's mobility
      advantage.
   2. Defensive Stalling: A Dice 6 guarding a Base with a Dice 5 nearby is almost impossible to break under
      v1.0 rules.
   3. Tempo vs. Math: The game is a "Race" in the early game (Dice 1 speed) but becomes a "Math Problem" in the
      late game (Armor vs. Attack).


  Recommendations for Future Iterations
   * Implement "Momentum": Allow units moving 3 hexes to gain +1 Attack. This lets a Dice 1 (Atk 2+1=3) kill a
     Dice 3.
   * Implement "David & Goliath": Give Dice 1 an automatic win against Dice 6 to prevent the "Immortal 6"
     stalemate.
   * Mutual Destruction: If Attack == Armor, both units should be removed. This facilitates board clearing and
     prevents "Safe Stalestacks."
