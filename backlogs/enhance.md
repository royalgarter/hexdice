### Recommended Rule Enhancements
To fix the stalemates and balance issues while maintaining the designer's vision of a deterministic, dice-based tactical game, we recommend implementing the following changes:

**1. Replace "Roll Setup" with "Draft Setup"**
*   *Current Rule:* Players roll all their dice and are stuck with the results (with minor rerolls).
*   *New Rule:* Both players roll their allotted dice into a shared pool in the center of the table. Players then take turns drafting one die at a time to build their army, placing them in their deployment zones. 
*   *Why:* This eliminates setup RNG and introduces a highly strategic pre-game phase. Players can counter-draft based on what their opponent is building.

**2. Introduce "Flanking" to Break Stalemates**
*   *New Rule:* If an attacking unit engages a defender that is already adjacent to at least one other friendly unit, the attacker gains **+1 Attack** for that combat calculation.
*   *Why:* In a deterministic game, you need spatial positioning to overcome mathematical deficits. Flanking rewards players for maneuvering and surrounding Tankers, breaking the "turtle" strategy without relying on random dice rolls.

**3. Adjust the "Reroll" Penalty**
*   *Current Rule:* Rerolling a die drops its Effective Armor to 0 until the next turn, making it an easy target.
*   *New Rule:* A rerolled unit suffers a **-1 Armor penalty** (minimum 0) until its next activation, rather than dropping completely to 0. 
*   *Why:* Dropping to 0 armor is a death sentence, making the Reroll action practically unusable in the mid-game. A -1 penalty makes it a calculated tactical risk rather than a guaranteed loss of a unit.

**4. Nerf the "Guard" Action**
*   *Current Rule:* Guard provides +1 Armor AND completely absorbs one attack without the defender taking Armor Reduction.
*   *New Rule:* Guard provides +1 Effective Armor. If an attack fails against a Guarding unit, both units still take 1 Armor Reduction, and the Guard status is removed.
*   *Why:* The current Guard rule makes Tankers nearly invincible and heavily disincentivizes attacking. Allowing Armor Reduction to still apply (even if the attack fails) ensures that concentrated fire will eventually break through a defensive line.
